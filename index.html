Process

Intersectional Interests

My usual process combines a love of hard deadlines with design thinking.

My approach to design thinking is often influenced by four backgrounds:

3D Experience Design

My first love is producing a good experience by adapting existing practices to new forms. Whether working on Content or UX, I use experience with wordsmithing, immersive theater, frame composition, visual editing, and multi-linear gamification. (I think game design is underrated.)

Solutions Architecture

I am not an engineer, but I speak engineer well enough to have learned what's easy to code, and what isn't. One of the first things I ask during ideation:
"When has there been a similar problem to this in the past?" Analogizing from past cases helps present obvious solutions to seemingly new problems.

Intuitive Communication

There's no straight line to making something intuitive. Measuring intuitivity is grounded in measuring how effectively your teaching facilitates comprehension. The solutions are different whether you're designing a webpage, image, video, VR experience, mobile game, theater production, etc. But the goal is the same:
"Do people get it?"

Collaborative Management

If you want to avoid blindspots, you need to get different perspectives. At every step, I encourage feedback from beyond the designers, engineers, stakeholders and others that make the product. "Can we go to our users?" "What other opinions can we get?" I never decide by committee, but surprising solutions come from surprising places.
